Tool: Sequence Viewer

The Sequence Viewer displays individual sequences and sequence alignments of amino acids and nucleotides, with crosstalk to any associated structures. A sequence file can be opened with the command open, and the sequence of a structure chain can be shown by clicking a link in the Log (in the Chain information table that appears when a structure is opened) or by using the command:

sequence chain  chain-spec

Multiple Sequence Viewer windows can be shown at the same time, and they can be manipulated like other panels in ChimeraX (more...).

Sequence-Structure Association

A sequence that arose from a structure's chain information is automatically associated with that chain, with light yellow and light green boxes indicating protein helices and strands, respectively. Such sequences are closed when their structure chains are deleted or closed, and they are not automatically associated with other structures (see below).

When sequences and structures are opened independently, a structure chain will be associated automatically with a sequence if their sequences can be aligned without mismatches exceeding 1/10 of the residues in the structure chain. Associations are reported in the Log. For automatic association, gaps in the structure sequence relative to the sequence in the alignment file can only occur where residues are missing from the structure (for example, a flexible loop with insufficient density for coordinates to be determined). Mismatches are indicated with red boxes, missing residues with black outline boxes. If a mismatch or missing residue only occurs in a subset of multiple associated structures, a pink box or gray outline is shown instead.

Further effects of association:

  • a box colored to match the associated model is shown around the sequence name, or a dark-green dashed outline box if chains from multiple models are associated
  • pausing the cursor over the sequence name reports the association, and over residues in the sequence window reports the corresponding structure residue(s)
  • dragging a box in the sequence window selects the corresponding structure residues
  • selections (by any means) of the structure are shown as green boxes around the corresponding residues in the sequence window
