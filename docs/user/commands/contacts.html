Command: contacts

Usage:
contacts  atom-spec  [ probeRadius  rad ] [ areaCutoff  area ]

The contacts command calculates the buried solvent-accessible surface area (SASA) for each pair of chains in the specified set of atoms, based on chain IDs, and generates a network diagram of the interchain contacts. Unspecified atoms are ignored, but solvent, ions, and ligand residues are not excluded automatically. The SASA is calculated as described for buriedarea, and the measured interface areas are reported in the Log. See also: sasa

Whereas the surface command shows solvent-excluded surfaces (SES) defined by the closest approach of the surface of a probe sphere rolled over the atomic VDW spheres, the solvent-accessible surface (SAS) is traced out by the center of the probe sphere and is not displayed. The probeRadius rad is the radius of this “rolling sphere” probe (default 1.4 Å, the value often used to approximate a water molecule). Interfaces with at least area buried (default 300 Å2) are counted as contacts and shown as lines in the diagram.

Chain-Chain Network Diagram

In the 2D network diagram, the nodes (circles) represent chains, and the edges (lines) between nodes represent chain-chain interfaces. Node colors match the predominant colors of the chains when the diagram is created, and node sizes are proportional to chain surface areas. Chain-chain interfaces with buried area less than half of the largest interface in the structure are depicted with dashed lines.
