Command: buriedarea

Usage:
buriedarea  atom-spec1  withAtoms2  atom-spec2  [ probeRadius  rad ] [ listResidues ] [ cutoffArea  area ] [ select ] [ color  color-spec ]

The buriedarea command calculates the buried solvent-accessible surface area between two sets of atoms, defined as:

½ (sasa1 + sasa2 – sasa12)
where  sasa1 is the area of the solvent-accessible surface (SAS) enclosing the atoms in  atom-spec1,  sasa2 is the area of the SAS enclosing the atoms in  atom-spec2, and  sasa12 is the area of the SAS enclosing both sets of atoms together. All of the measured areas are reported in the Log. The sets of atoms should be specified with care; they should not overlap, and solvent, ions, and ligand residues are not excluded automatically. Unspecified atoms are ignored. See also: sasa

Whereas the surface command shows solvent-excluded surfaces (SES) defined by the closest approach of the surface of a probe sphere rolled over the spacefilling atoms, the solvent-accessible surface (SAS) is traced out by the center of the probe sphere and is not displayed. The probeRadius rad is the radius of this “rolling sphere” probe (default 1.4 Å, the value often used to approximate a water molecule).

Residues with at least cutoffArea area buried (default 1.0 Å2) can be:

  • listed along with their buried areas in the Log using the listResidues option
  • selected with the select option
  • colored using color color-spec

The buried area of a residue is its SAS area in the individual set minus that in the combined set.

Examples:

buriedarea /c & protein with /d & protein
– calculate buried surface area between the protein parts only of chains C and D
buried ligand with protein list sel
– select and list residues with ≥ 1.0 Å2 area buried between ligand and protein
