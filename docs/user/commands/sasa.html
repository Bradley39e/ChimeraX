Command: sasa

Usage:
sasa  atom-spec1  [ probeRadius  rad ] [ sum  atom-spec2 ]

The sasa command calculates a solvent-accessible surface enclosing the atoms in  atom-spec1 and reports the total surface area in the Log. In addition, the area contribution from some subset of the atoms (given as  atom-spec2) can be obtained with the sum option. The surface is not displayed. The atoms should be specified with care; solvent, ions, and ligand residues are not excluded automatically. Unspecified atoms are ignored, as are atoms in  atom-spec2 that are not also in  atom-spec1. See also: buriedarea

Whereas the surface command shows solvent-excluded surfaces (SES) defined by the closest approach of the surface of a probe sphere rolled over the spacefilling atoms, the solvent-accessible surface (SAS) is traced out by the center of the probe sphere and is not displayed. The probeRadius rad is the radius of this “rolling sphere” probe (default 1.4 Å, the value often used to approximate a water molecule).

Example:

sasa #1/a & protein sum :phe,tyr,trp
– calculate the SAS of the protein in model #1 chain A and report both the total area and the contribution from phenylalanine, tyrosine, and tryptophan residues
