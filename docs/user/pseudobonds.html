Pseudobonds

A pseudobond is drawn between two atoms to signify a connection other than a covalent bond, such as a hydrogen bond, metal coordination bond, or missing peptide segment. A pseudobond model is a named set of pseudobonds that can be treated collectively. Pseudobonds can be defined arbitrarily by creating and opening a pseudobond file. See also: hbonds, rungs, struts, crosslinks, display hierarchy

Pseudobonds are only displayed when both of their endpoint atoms are displayed. For purposes of pseudobond display, backbone atoms are considered as displayed when their display is being suppressed by cartoon (ribbon).

Commands that act on pseudobonds include color, style, and show. The command-line specifiers hbonds and pbonds refer to hydrogen-bond pseudobonds and all pseudobonds, respectively, as well as their endpoint atoms. Certain commands (color, show) may require using the target p option to indicate acting on pseudobonds and not their flanking atoms.
